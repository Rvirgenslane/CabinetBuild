Here is the refined, human-readable summary for your **two-cabinet stack**.

## 1. The Master Dimensions (The "Box")
Each of your two cabinets is a mirrored twin.
* **Outside Width:** 35.5"
* **Outside Height:** 38.75"
* **Outside Depth:** 12.25"
* **The Joinery:** The top and bottom panels are "tucked" into **1/8" deep rabbets** on the side panels. This means the top/bottom boards are **34.25" wide**.

---

## 2. The Door Plan (Shaker Style)
You have **4 doors total** (2 per cabinet). Each door is designed as a "Full Overlay" (covering the face frame).

### The Frame (3/4" Solid Maple)
* **Stiles (Vertical):** 2 per door. Cut these to exactly **38.625" long**.
* **Rails (Horizontal):** 2 per door. Cut these to **13.0625" long**. 
    * *Note: This length accounts for the 2.25" frame width on both sides, plus a 1/4" "tenon" or "tongue" that sticks into the stiles.*
* **The Groove:** On the inside edges of all these pieces, cut a **1/4" wide x 1/4" deep groove**.

### The Panel (1/4" Plywood)
* **Panel Size:** **13.5625" x 34.625"**.
* **The Fit:** The panel slides into the grooves of the frame. 
* **The Reveal:** Push the panel toward the front so it sits **1/8" recessed** from the face of the maple. This creates the "Shadow Line."

## 4. Hardware & Assembly
* **Hinges:** Use "Full Overlay" concealed (European) hinges. Since your doors are nearly 39" tall, use **3 hinges per door** to prevent warping.
* **The Stack:** When you put the top cabinet on the bottom one, use 1-1/4" screws to pull the two "floors" together.
* **The Backer:** Your 1/4" backer sits in that **3/8" deep rabbet** we coded earlier. This leaves a 1/8" gap behind the cabinet so it sits flat against the wall even if your walls aren't perfectly straight.
